Where & When was Basketball invented?

Where?

It was invented in Springfield, Massachusetts.

When?

1891

By who?

James Naismith

Why?

It was too cold to play outside so he made a sport to play inside.

How do you play basketball?

What do you need?

You need a basketball hoop, and a basketball. Originally they used a bucket as a hoop.

What is the Objective of the game

The objective is to win the game. To win, your team needs more points than the opponents team.

Points

How do you earn points?

There is a three point line. Inside the three point line if you shoot (unless it's a free-throw, explained later), it is worth two points. If you shoot outside the line, it is worth three points. You shoot free-throws if there is a foul (more on that explained later). If you make a bucket from either the three point line or inside the three point line, you will get points, 3 and 2 respectively.

How long do you have to score the points?

Regulation time is 48 minutes, split into four quarters, with each quarter being 12 minutes. If the score is tied after regulation, there will be an overtime (ot) for 5 minutes. If the game is still tied, then there will be ot-2, and that keeps going until a team wins after the 5 minutes in ot.

Fouls

Types of fouls

Regular Foul

A regular foul is some small contact. If that team has 5 Team Fouls (A Team Foul is the amount of fouls a team has in a quarter) or more, the person who was fouled will get two free-throws. If a player has 6 fouls, it is out for the rest of the game.

Shooting Foul

Shooting Fouls are similar to Regular Fouls, except it's in a shooting motion. If you do a shooting foul, the opponent gets the amount of free-throws as points he/she would've made if the shot would've made. If the shot made, and you did a shooting foul, then your opponent gets the points for the buckets and one free-throw.

Technical Fouls

You can get a Technical Foul when you scream at the referee/official. If you do a Technical Foul, the opposing team gets to shoot one free-throw. Anyone on the team currently on the field can shoot those free-throws. A team's coach can also be called for a Technical Foul if he/she screams at the referee/official. If you get two Technical Fouls in a game, you're ejected (Even coaches can get ejected). In the NBA, for each 16 technical fouls you do per season, you are not allowed to play for one game.

Flagrant Fouls

There are two different types of Flagrant Fouls: Flagrant I and Flagrant II:

Flagrant I

Flagrant Foul I's are when a player does unnecessary contact. If you get two Flagrant I's, you're ejected.

Flagrant II

Flagrant Foul II's are when a player does unnecessary and excessive contact. If you do a Flagrant I, you're ejected for the rest of the game.

What is the NBA?

The NBA (National Basketball Association) is made up of 30 teams, 15 in each conference. Each season has 82 games. In both confernces, the top 6 will make the playoffs. The 7th-10th seeds will play in the play-in tournament. I will tell you more information on the play-in tournament below. Then there is the playoffs. I will also explain to you about those later.

NBA Play-in Tournaments

The NBA Play-in Tournaments are best of one series. The 6th to 10th seeds of both confernces play to find the 7th and 8th seed for the playoffs. 7th plays 8th in the 7th Seed Game, 9th plays 10th in the Eliminator, and the Loser of the 7th Seed Game plays the winner of the Eliminator for the 8th seed game. The winner of the 7th seed game and the 8th seed game get the 7th and 8th seed, respectively. After that, the playoffs start.

Playoffs

I will explain you the playoffs as the four rounds. All playoff series are best of 7 games. The better record team has Game 1 & 2 at home, then worse record team has 3 & 4, then best record team has Game 5, worse record has Game 6, and best record have Game 7. Game 5-7 are all if needed.

First Round

In the first round, the series are (in each conference) 1st VS 8th [1st Seed Series], 2nd VS 7th [2nd Seed Series], 3rd VS 6th [3rd Seed Series], and 4th VS 5th [4th Seed Series]. The winners of the series will advance to the Confernce Semi-Finals. The losers will be eliminated.

Conference Semi-Finals

The Conference Semi-Finals have two series in each conference. The series in the Conference Semi-Finals are Winner of 1st Seed Series VS Winner of 4th Seed Series(Conference Semi-Finals 1), and Winner of 2nd Seed Series VS Winner of 3rd Seed Series (Conference Semi-Finals 2). The losers will be eliminated.

Conference Finals

There will be two Conference Finals Series: 1 in the Western Conference and 1 in the Eastern Conference. In each conference, the series will be Winner of the Conference Semi-Finals 1 VS Winner of Conference Semi-Finals 2. The winners become the conference champions, and advance to the NBA Finals, while the losers are eliminated.

NBA Finals

The winner of the NBA Finals is the World Champion. The winners of both conference will be facing each-other off in this Best-of-7 Series. The winner will be crowned the NBA Champion, while the loser will be crowned the Runner's Up!

About NBA Players

LeBron James

LeBron James is said to be the best player currently in the NBA. He started playing without going t college, and came into the 2003 NBA Draft. He was drafted as the first overall pick by the Cleveland Cavaliers, and stayed there for 6 seasons. Then he moved to the Heat in 2010 and won a couple of championships there. After winning he came back to the Cleveland Cavaliers, where he won a Championship in 2016. They went to the finals four years in a row, from 2015 all the way until 2018, and all of them were against the Golden State Warriors. The Cleveland Cavaliers were 1-3. In 2018, LeBron moved to the LA Lakers to hopefully win a championship there, which he did. In 2020, him, along with Anthony Davis, won a championship inside the bubble (due to COVID-19). After that, though, LeBron hasn't been performing as well and maybe is losing his position as the best player currently in the NBA. Coming into the 2022 season, the Lakers had many NBA Greats, including Russel Westbrook, Carmelo Anthony, Anthony Davis, and more, but couldn't even make the playoffs.

Stephen Curry

Stephen Curry is one of the best NBA Players in NBA history, and the best shooter in NBA history. He was drafted in 2009 by the Golden State Warriors as the 7th pick, which was considered lucky for Warriors at the time and now as Chris Mullin, one of the greatest players the Warriors had drafted before, was a 7th pick. As a rookie, Stephen Curry did awesome, and did as good in the next few years. His buddies, Klay Thompson and Draymond Green were drafted 2 and 3 years later respectively. In 2013, they made the Playoffs and started their dynasty. In the 5 years, from 2015-2019, they made the NBA Championship all 5 times, and won 3 of them. Even though Stephen Curry never got Finals MVP, he was the main reason the Warriors won according to many many people. Why? His shooting. Stephen Curry's shooting was one of the best you could see. He introduced the Half-court shot to the NBA, and has made many deeper shots. In 2015 and 2016 he got MVP. In 2020, when the Warriors were the worst team in the league, he was injured most of the time. In 2021, he single-handedly led the Warriors to the 8th seed, but they lost in the Play-in round to the Grizzlies and didn't make the playoffs. In 2022 though, Stephen Curry carried his team, along with Klay Thompson and Andrew Wiggins, and they won the 2022 NBA Championship. He also for his first ever Finals MVP in 2022, adding to what people called, "the missing piece" of Steph's Trophy Cabinet.

Kevin Durant

Drafted as the second pick for the Supersonics in the 2007 NBA draft, Kevin Durant was a star. He got Rookie of the Year, and in 2010 became an all star. The Supersonics became the Thunder, and Durant, along with Rusell Westbrook & James Harden led them to a NBA Final appearance, but lost there. In the 2016 offseason, after the Warriors went 73-9 and lost in the Finals, Durant came to the Warriors to join Klay Thompson, Stephen Curry, and Draymond Green. KD won finals MVP in 2017 & 2018 as he carried the Warriors. In 2019, he suffered a big injury in Game 5 of the NBA Finals, when the Warriors were down 3-1. That was the game he came back from a smaller injury, and after scoring a little bit, he suffered another one. He was out for a year after that, and the Warriors ended up losing 4-2. After that, he moved to the Nets with DeAndre Jordan & Kyrie Irving. In the 2020-2021 season, James Harden joined the Nets. They still lost to the Milwaukee Bucks 4-3 in Game 7 of the Eastern Conference Semi-Finals. In 2022, Durant was injured in the middle of the season as well. But as Harden was traded to the 76ers, it was only him & Irving. They didn't do that well, and ended up getting swept by the Boston Celtics in the first round of the 2022 NBA Playoffs.
